From the PEP and NO_PEP crosstabs and visualizations, we see:

  • slightly less than half of the sampled people participated (~45%)
  • men were slightly more likely to invest in a Personal Equity Plan
  • regional patterns appear similar between PEP and NO_PEP, indicating region probably isn't an predictive factor
  • mortgage utilization appears similar between PEP and NO_PEP, indicating mortgage utilization is also not a very predictive factor
  • PEP participants were much more likely to be unmarried than PEP abstainers, possibly implying marriage and related factors reduce the availability of funds for investment, or perhaps marriage leads to funds being invested in different accounts (eg college, home downpayment, etc), or perhaps people with more available funds are less interested in marriage. More research would be needed to address this phenomenon.
